03 / Personal Research

Bitcoin Cycle StudyA self-directed research project

2024 – Present · Personal documentation · No live capital

An ongoing study of Bitcoin's historical price cycles, written up as a personal reference document. The work covers cycle measurement, position-sizing math, capital allocation rules, and — most usefully — a written self-criticism of every assumption the framework makes. It is documentation of how I think, not a claim that I have solved the market.

My Role

Sole author. Researched, drafted, and revised the methodology over multiple iterations. The current document is its third version, written specifically to survive its own criticism.

A small sample size (six historical cycles) limits any forward claim. The document treats this honestly. Available on request for finance mentors and university reviewers.

About

The person
behind the work

I'm Mahardika Lambang Sugiharto, a Class XI student at SMKN 2 Pekalongan, expecting to graduate in 2027. I study Broadcasting & Film by curriculum, and I study markets by choice.

What ties the two together is a method, not a topic: study slowly, document everything in writing, and pre-commit to rules before emotions can interfere. A film set runs that way. A trade plan should run that way. The principle is the same.

I prefer to under-promise and let the work argue for itself. If something isn't ready, this site says so. If something is uncertain, this site says that too. I'd rather earn trust slowly than borrow credibility I haven't built.
